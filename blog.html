YOUTH: THE PHENOMENAL BRAIN OF START-UPS.

In this tech-savvy generation of gadgets and smart devices, each one of is associated with technology in some form or the other. We may use different browsers, go through various websites, or use a number of applications in our smartphones. Certainly, the invasion of technical trends in our country has changed the scenario of industries and the corporate world in a great sense. It is quite evident nowadays to watch kids using smartphones and other high-tech devices smoothly. The technology has enabled each one of us to easily do whatever we want, be it personal or professional. It is getting smarter, faster and better. But ever wondered how these technologies that we use on routine basis are developed? Who is the mastermind? And what it takes to get into such technical advancements? Well, we have the answers for you right here.


But what’s bothersome today is that India might be an early adopter of technology on the commercial front but not when it comes to educating the masses, especially the youth; about the technicalities of the services and devices they are using. The education system does not pay much heed to it. India is the second largest smartphone user after China, but why is it that the youngsters mostly invest in using smartphones and all the trending technologies and not in developing or knowing the basics of how to make it work. Or is it that we might think of doing so but feel it’s better to go with the flow? This skill-based brain is what lacks in today’s educated masses. It is maybe because of the education system that has trained us to think more logically than creatively. Our brains have been trained from the very beginning to be merely a good logical thinker than to get creative and explore new paths. There are a large number of colleges in India which claim world-class education facilities for students, but this is the one and the only superficial reality of our education system. In the name of education, the students are only imparted knowledge on the theoretical front and its applications still remain to be conserved only to specific organizations and education authorities. The fact is that technical studies are limited even in the largest of colleges in India. Our perception is shaped so as to consider a regular 9 to 5 job as the source of income for most educated people in our country. As students, we only study what our teachers teach us in colleges. We do not do anything creative about it, never try to think out of the box.


We are just running after getting placed in highly paid companies. Nowadays what we are taught in colleges and universities is the basics of programming languages which won’t suffice for us to work in corporate sector.


Psychologically speaking, our brain has two segments namely the left half of the brain which deals with logical & analytical aspects and the right half which deals with aspects of creativity, imagination, intuition and thought. Every college curriculum lets our left-brain function but what about the other half which lets us think differently and nowadays every other graduate is running on the same track. If we take an example of startups, we might think of it at some point in life but only a few succeed in building up one. What do you think is the reason behind it? As a part of syllabus all technical field students are taught programming languages & I’m pretty sure that most of you are aware of them but those of you knowing them to the core and aware with it’s application real world might be small in number. The unprofessional force does not create room for competition for existing companies and also there is not much risk of competition for them. This is one big factor which gives the companies and employers a chance to convince the unskilled students to join internships and collaborate with them. They claim to train them completely but the harsh reality is that this loophole in our education system is taken advantage of by these industries to make money from students. Students join training sessions and courses yet only a considerable knowledge is given to them. This is the reason why future startups are quite limited when it comes to their success. Thus, the scenario almost remains constant and there comes no revolution in the mindset of youngsters. Perceptions like these must change now.


If you, for once, keep aside your job-oriented perception and think of doing a start-up at your own, won’t it be fascinating to see a company by your own name, own policies and the fame, acclaim all yours? What is required to do so, is training, smart work and lots of investment of effort and time. Try learning something that is off the beat for example, why not try making a website of your own than just browsing one? The curiosity, passion, and motivation have to be instilled from within and then, slowly and steadily this nation will become a place of future developers of technology, rather than that of people merely marveling about them. Everyone wants easy money today. Some lack knowledge while others lack passion to do something creative. Moreover, those who have ample knowledge, do not know what can be done with it. This is the reason why most startups fail and as a result of failure, people switch to jobs. Just imagine if failures took to inventors like Edison, Einstein, and Newton, this world would have been a different place today. So, switch to the life mantra that says “More the failure, more will be our opportunities for success” and your perception on life will be changed altogether. Success comes only to those who are dedicated and pour honesty in their work.


Ever heard of AIBrain or DeepMind? Of course, you may be much aware of it. It is the most accurate and successful artificial intelligent system. Similarly, Internet of Things (IoT), which is a popularly emerging technology nowadays. All the big industries & data processing companies have started exploring the scopes of IoT into their product. But certainly, it might not be a part of the college/institutional curriculum till now. Recently, the use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the websites, web development, and apps has been considered by technical enthusiasts. There are also various popular tools for implementing AI into applications like Caffe, Microsoft Cognitive Tool, Deeplearning4J, Mahout, H2O.ai and many more. Again, this is something we are not taught at our graduation or higher levels. Take a look at the evolution of JavaScript, Python, and the much burning topic of Tesla Hyperloop by Elon musk.


Talking about the latest advancements in technology today, the users may be limited in number because of the fact that we are only taught about technology that evolved years ago and not about the present scenario. There is framework technology available for most languages today, different ways to create responsive websites using Bootstrap, Laravel to name a few. Not just the contemporary scenario but these emerging technologies will surely have a great scope in the coming decades and have wide applications.

So, programmers and computer geeks must gear up and start learning on their own with an aim to work for themselves and not under someone who uses your skills and labor to benefit their organizational profits. Now it is high time to get off this notion of static jobs and skill ourselves to work independently as leaders of our own setups because opportunities open arms to embrace only those who can identify their talents and work towards their goals. So, we are the future of technology in the coming years, we must invest in self-learning so that we can revolutionize develop a better India tomorrow. It is rightly said “Better an Oops than a What If” because it is worth giving a try at something than fearing failure and doing nothing about it. What is your take on it?
